My Process

01.

Discover

Audit, Research & Benchmarking

I start by deconstructing the landscape. For redesigns, I perform a Heuristic Audit to pinpoint where the current UI fails. For new products, I use AI-assisted synthesis to cluster user insights and competitive patterns. I don't just collect screenshots; I interpret them to find the "why" before I touch a canvas.

02.

Define

Problem Framing & Strategic Flows

I translate raw research into a design strategy. This phase is about Information Architecture and high-level user flows. I define the constraints and the "Job to be Done," ensuring that the visual work that follows is anchored in a clear solution for a specific user pain point.

03.

Design

Lo-fi → Logic Validation → Hi-fi

I begin with wireframes to nail the hierarchy and layout. Before moving to high-fidelity, I perform Logic Validation — stress-testing the "unhappy paths," edge cases, and complex state changes. Once the logic is bulletproof, I apply the visual layer, focusing on typography, spacing, and brand expression.

04.

Validate

Prototyping & User Testing

I build interactive prototypes to put my assumptions to the test. Through task-based usability sessions, I observe how real users interact with the interface. This isn't just about finding bugs; it's about refining the friction points and iterating on the UI until the experience feels intuitive.

05.

Deliver

Handoff & Implementation Support

Design doesn't end at the Figma file. I provide developers with organised Design Tokens, documented component states, and accessibility specs. I stay involved during the build for Design QA, ensuring that the final coded product matches the intended craft and quality of the design.
